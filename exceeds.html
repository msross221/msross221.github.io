Voter Categories and Divisions by Voter Education and Voter Income

This parallel categories plot shows the relationship between how often a voter votes (voter category), the level of education of that voter, and the income category of that voter. As shown in the visualization, the majority of voters who always vote have a college education and make above $75k a year. There is a different pattern shown for voters who rarely or never vote. The majority of these voters have a high school education or less and make less than $40k a year. There is no clear pattern for people who vote sporadically.


Percent of Voter Turnout and Percent of Absentee Voters

This bubble plot shows the relationship between voter turnout and the percent of voters who used absentee ballots over time between 2014 and 2022. Generally, voter turnout increases over time, as does the percent of voters who vote absentee. 2022 is the year with the most notable change in the percent of voters voting absentee. In 2016 and 2022 there were congressional and presidential elections, which is reflected in a higher percent of voter turnout in those years.


What does voter turnout look like in Virginia cities and counties in the 2022 Virginia general election?

As shown by the map, there is generally higher voter turnout in counties that are in the northern and eastern part of Virginia. The western most counties have the lowest voter turnout. Furthermore, it appears that cities tend to have higher voter turnout than counties do.
